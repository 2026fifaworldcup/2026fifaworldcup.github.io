About this site

2026 World Cup is an unofficial tournament reference by Studio Nani. The goal is simple: make the expanded 48-team format easier to scan for people who want groups, standings, fixtures, Round of 32 paths, and country squad guides in one lightweight page.

The home page focuses on the complete group-stage view and the route into the knockout bracket. Country pages provide a quick guide to each team’s group slot, likely opponents, recent form notes, player watch sections, and links back to the bracket. The Round of 32 page explains how group winners, runners-up, and best third-placed teams feed into the knockout stage.

This is a fan and information project. It is not affiliated with FIFA, the FIFA World Cup, national football associations, official broadcasters, sponsors, or tournament organizers. Team names and competition names are used only to identify public sporting information.

What visitors can use it for

  • Checking the 2026 World Cup group draw and each country’s slot.
  • Following standings, points, wins, draws, losses, goals, and advancement lines.
  • Understanding the Round of 32 bracket path before the knockout teams are finalized.
  • Opening quick country guides for major teams, host nations, and qualified sides.
  • Switching between English and Korean search-friendly tournament summaries.

Data approach

Group and knockout information is maintained from public tournament references and is updated as confirmed information changes. Before final squads are announced, squad pages are treated as public-reference guides rather than official final lists. When official tournament details change, the site may update page titles, descriptions, tables, and country summaries to keep the guide useful.

Because fixtures, squads, injuries, disciplinary records, and kickoff details can change close to matchday, visitors should verify critical information with official organizers, federations, or broadcasters before making travel, ticketing, or betting decisions.
